Things to do near Uzès

A week near La Vallette fills itself. Here is how guests actually spend their days — from the obvious to the things worth knowing about.

The essentials

Within fifteen minutes

5 min · Wednesday & Saturday

Saturday market, Uzès

The Place aux Herbes fills with the region's best producers. Cheese, charcuterie, truffle, olive oil, ceramics, textiles. Arrive before 10h for the full experience.

15 min · Year round

Pont du Gard

The Roman aqueduct at sunrise, before the coaches arrive, is one of the better things southern France offers. Scale, silence and two thousand years of engineering in one view.

10 min · June to September

Swimming in the Gardon

The Gardon river has clear, cold water and natural pools that aren't easy to find without local knowledge. We include directions in the apartment. A half-day well spent.

5 min · All year

Uzès old town

The medieval centre of Uzès — the Duché, the towers, the arcaded squares — rewards an unhurried morning. Less visited than the Luberon, better preserved than most.

12 min · Autumn harvest

Wine routes of the Gard

The Costières de Nîmes and Duché d'Uzès appellations run through the hills around La Vallette. Several estates accept visitors for tasting. We can recommend the ones worth stopping for.

A few km · During your stay

HAP Céramique, Sagriès

Charlotte Casanas runs a ceramic atelier a few minutes from the house. Functional objects and lighting made by hand in the south of France. Worth a visit even if you don't buy anything.

Worth the journey

Thirty to forty minutes away

Nîmes
30 min
The Roman city has the best-preserved arena in Europe — still in use for concerts and the twice-yearly Féria. The Maison Carrée temple and the Jardins de la Fontaine are worth combining into a half-day.
Avignon
40 min
The walled city, the Palais des Papes, the bridge. In July, the Festival d'Avignon takes over the city completely — one of the world's great theatre festivals, if you plan around it.
Gorges du Gardon
20 min
The canyon of the Gardon river between the Pont du Gard and Collias. Kayak hire at Collias, walking trails above the gorge, and more wild swimming spots than the tourist circuit finds. A full day if the weather is with you.
Saint-Rémy-de-Provence
50 min
The most refined market town in the western Provence. Wednesday morning market, excellent antique dealers, good restaurants. Combine with the Roman ruins at Glanum on the same trip.
By season

When to come, what to expect

Spring · April – June

The best season

The garrigue is in flower, temperatures are perfect, the crowds haven't arrived. The Saturday market is at its finest from April. Easiest time to visit the Pont du Gard without queuing.

Summer · July – August

Hot, full, festive

The Festival d'Avignon in July, the Féria de Nîmes in May and September, the Nuits Musicales d'Uzès in late July. River swimming is essential. Book everything in advance.

Autumn · Sept – Nov

Harvest season

The vintage in September, truffle season beginning in November, the second Féria de Nîmes in September. Fewer tourists, the same quality of light. One of the underrated times to visit.

19:30
