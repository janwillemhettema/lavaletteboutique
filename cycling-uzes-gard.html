Cycling near Uzès

The roads and tracks around Sagriès are among the quietest cycling terrain in the south of France. Garrigue plateau, limestone gorges, almost no traffic. A guide from the doorstep of La Vallette Boutique.

By the hosts of La Vallette Boutique, Sagriès, Gard

Why this area

Cycling terrain worth knowing about

The Gard is not a cycling destination in the way that Provence is. There are no famous climbs here. What there is instead is better: roads that go for thirty kilometres without a junction, garrigue scrubland that smells of wild thyme in the heat, gorges that most cyclists have never found, and terrain that suits every level.

From La Vallette in Sagriès you can roll out of the courtyard and be on empty roads within two minutes. The plateau above the Gardon gorge connects Uzès, the Pont du Gard and the Gorges du Gardon without touching a main road.

"Leave the house at 7h before the heat, take the plateau road east toward Collias, descend to the Gardon, cross at the bridge. You will not see another car for forty minutes."
La Vallette Boutique, Sagriès
Routes from the doorstep

Five routes from La Vallette

The Garrigue Loop
Easy 28 km180 m Road / hybrid
Sagriès east to Collias, north along the plateau above the Gardon, return via Uzès outskirts and back through the garrigue. Almost no traffic on the plateau roads. A 90-minute morning ride before a day doing something else.
Pont du Gard by Road
Moderate 42 km return280 m Road bike
South from Sagriès on the D18, through the garrigue plateau, dropping to the Gardon valley. The approach gives you the aqueduct from the road above before you descend. Lock the bike at the car park, swim, return the same way or via Remoulins.
Gorges du Gardon Circuit
Challenging 65 km620 m Road bike
North from Sagriès to Uzès, east on the plateau road, north along the rim of the Gorges du Gardon, descent to the gorge floor, south to the Pont du Gard, then back to La Vallette. The gorge section is the centrepiece: road running along the canyon rim with the Gardon visible below.
Uzès Market Morning
Easy 10 km return60 m Any bike
Five kilometres from La Vallette to the Place aux Herbes. Saturday morning: leave at 9h, arrive for the market, buy provisions, return before noon. Suitable for any bike, any rider, any age.
Gravel: Garrigue and Mas Track
Moderate 35 km320 m Gravel / MTB
Unpaved tracks through the garrigue plateau east of Sagriès. Pale limestone gravel through scrubland, past isolated farmhouses. The route passes the edge of the Gorges du Gardon at two points where the view opens without warning over the canyon.
Practical information

What you need to know

01

Bike hire in Uzès

Several hire options in the town centre, including e-bikes and road bikes. For serious road riding, bringing your own bike is the better option. Ask us for the current recommendation.

02

The heat

From mid-June to mid-September, temperatures above 35°C are normal. Start before 8h and plan to finish by noon. Two litres of water minimum for any ride over 25 km in summer.

03

Road surfaces

Main roads are well-surfaced. Minor D-roads through the garrigue range from excellent to unpredictably rough. Gravel tracks are firm limestone, good drainage, but check after heavy rain.

04

Traffic

The plateau roads between Sagriès, Collias and the gorge carry almost no traffic outside summer weekends. The road to Uzès is light. Avoid the N86 and N106 entirely.

05

Navigation

Komoot has the most complete track data for this area. The IGN 1:25000 map covers all routes described here. Google Maps misses many of the best tracks.

06

Storage at La Vallette

We have secure covered storage for bikes at the house. Arriving by train in Nîmes or Avignon with a bike? Let us know and we will help coordinate the transfer.
