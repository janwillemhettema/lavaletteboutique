Cycling near Uzès

The roads and tracks around Sagriès are among the quietest and most varied cycling terrain in the south of France. Garrigue plateau, limestone gorges, Roman roads, almost no traffic. A guide from the doorstep of La Vallette Boutique.

By the hosts of La Vallette Boutique, Sagriès, Gard

Why this area

Cycling terrain worth knowing about

The Gard is not a cycling destination in the way that Provence or the Vendée are. There are no famous climbs here, no cycling tourism infrastructure. What there is instead is better: roads that go for thirty kilometres without a junction, garrigue scrubland that smells of wild thyme in the heat, gorges that most touring cyclists have never found, and a terrain that suits every level from a relaxed family loop to a serious 100-kilometre road ride.

From La Vallette in Sagriès you can roll out of the courtyard and be on empty roads within two minutes. The plateau above the Gardon gorge is flat enough for an easy morning, technical enough for gravel bikes, and connected enough to reach Uzès, the Pont du Gard or the Gorges du Gardon without touching a main road.

The only thing the area lacks is a good bike shop. The nearest reliable one is in Uzès or Nîmes. Bring your own spares, or rent from a specialist in Uzès before you begin.

Curated routes

From the doorstep

These routes all start from or near La Vallette in Sagriès. Distance and elevation are approximate. All are described for road bikes or hybrids unless noted as gravel.

The Garrigue Loop
Easy 28 km 180 m elevation Road / hybrid

The introductory ride. Sagriès east to Collias, north along the plateau above the Gardon, return via Uzès outskirts and back through the garrigue to La Vallette. Almost no traffic on the plateau roads. The descent to Collias is short and fast; the climb back up is ten minutes at modest gradient.

In spring the garrigue is in flower and the scent, wild thyme, rosemary, cistus, is extraordinary. In July and August, start by 7h30 and be back before the heat arrives. This route takes 90 minutes at a relaxed pace and makes an excellent morning before a day doing something else.

Pont du Gard by Road
Moderate 42 km return 280 m elevation Road bike

South from Sagriès on the D18, through the garrigue plateau, dropping to the Gardon valley and arriving at the Pont du Gard from the north. The approach gives you the aqueduct from the road above before you descend to the riverside, a reveal that parking arrivals miss entirely.

Lock the bike at the Rive Gauche car park, swim, have lunch, return the same way or via Remoulins for a different profile. The return leg climbs more than the outward, but the grades are gentle and the plateau road is fast. Allow three and a half hours for the full loop including a stop at the water.

Gorges du Gardon Circuit
Challenging 65 km 620 m elevation Road bike

The serious ride. North from Sagriès to Uzès, east on the plateau road, north along the rim of the Gorges du Gardon to Saint-Hilaire-d'Ozilhan, descent to the gorge floor, south along the river to the Pont du Gard, then back west and north to La Vallette. The gorge section is the centrepiece: the road runs along the canyon rim with the Gardon visible below through the garrigue.

The climb out of the gorge at Saint-Hilaire is the hardest moment, 4 km at 6 percent average. Everything else is manageable. Take water for the gorge section; there are no facilities on the canyon road. Allow four and a half to five hours.

Uzès Market Morning
Easy 10 km return 60 m elevation Any bike

Five kilometres from La Vallette to the Place aux Herbes in Uzès, the simplest, most useful ride on this list. Saturday morning: leave at 9h, arrive for the market, buy provisions, attach them to the bike, return before noon. The road from Sagriès is largely flat with one small rise. Suitable for any bike, any rider, any age.

The return with full panniers or a backpack from the market is one of the better morning activities available from La Vallette. Coffee on the Place aux Herbes terrace, bread and cheese for the rest of the day.

Gravel: Garrigue & Mas Track
Moderate 35 km 320 m elevation Gravel / MTB

For gravel bikes or light mountain bikes. The tracks through the garrigue plateau east of Sagriès are unpaved but well-maintained by agricultural traffic, pale limestone gravel through scrubland, past isolated mas farmhouses, with the occasional descent to a shepherd's track that appears on no digital map.

The route passes the edge of the Gorges du Gardon at two points where the view opens without warning over the canyon. Navigation is needed; the tracks connect to the roads in unexpected places. Download a GPX to a head unit or follow the IGN 1:25000 map for the Uzès area. This is the ride for those who want the landscape without other cyclists.

GPX files available on Komoot by searching "Sagriès garrigue gravel". Confirm track conditions with us before going, some sections close during dry periods due to fire risk.
Practical information

What you need to know

01

Bike hire in Uzès

Several hire options in the town centre, including e-bikes and road bikes. Quality varies, ask us for the current recommendation when you book. For serious road riding, bringing your own bike remains the better option.

02

The heat

From mid-June to mid-September, temperatures above 35°C are normal. Start before 8h and plan to be off the bike by noon. The garrigue holds heat and provides no shade. Two litres of water minimum for any ride over 25 km in summer.

03

Road surface

The main roads in the Gard are well-surfaced. The minor D-roads through the garrigue range from excellent to unpredictably rough. The tracks on the gravel routes are firm limestone, generally good drainage, but check after heavy rain.

04

Traffic

The plateau roads between Sagriès, Collias and the gorge carry almost no traffic outside summer weekends. The road to Uzès (D23) has light traffic throughout the week. Avoid the N86 and N106 entirely, there is no reason to be on them.

05

Navigation

The IGN 1:25000 map «Uzès» covers all routes described here. On a device, Komoot has the most complete track data for this area. Google Maps and Apple Maps miss many of the best tracks.

06

Storage at La Vallette

We have secure covered storage for bikes at the house. If you are arriving by train in Nîmes or Avignon with a bike, let us know and we will help coordinate the transfer. The TGV bike reservation system requires advance booking.

By season

When to cycle here

Spring, April to June
Best season
The finest cycling conditions of the year. Temperatures between 18 and 28°C. The garrigue is in bloom, the scent on an empty plateau road in May is worth the journey alone. The tracks are firm after winter rain. No heat risk before 10h. This is the reason to plan a cycling trip to this area.
Summer, July to August
Possible, but requires discipline. Start no later than 7h30. Be finished by noon. The heat on the garrigue in August is serious and the plateau provides no shade. That said, an early morning ride followed by a swim in the Gardon is one of the better combinations the area offers in summer.
Autumn, September to November
September and October are excellent, the heat eases, the light changes quality, and the garrigue takes on different colours. Some tracks may be temporarily closed in September due to residual fire risk from summer. November is fine for short rides but the days are short and rain becomes a factor.
Winter, December to March
The roads are quiet and the landscape is clear. Temperatures are mild by northern European standards, 10 to 15°C most days. Rain is more frequent and some gravel tracks become muddy. Not the destination for a cycling holiday, but a fine place for a clear-day ride during a longer winter stay.
