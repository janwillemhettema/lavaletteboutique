Visiting the Pont du Gard

Two thousand years old, 49 metres high, and still the most impressive single thing in the south of France. A guide from people who live fifteen minutes away.

By the hosts of La Vallette Boutique, Sagriès, Gard

What it is

Two thousand years of engineering

The Pont du Gard is a Roman aqueduct bridge built in the first century AD to carry water from a spring near Uzès to the Roman city of Nîmes, fifty kilometres away. It is the highest surviving Roman aqueduct bridge in the world, and one of the best-preserved monuments from antiquity in Europe. UNESCO listed it as a World Heritage Site in 1985.

What photographs do not convey is the scale. The three-tiered arcade rises 49 metres above the Gardon river. The construction precision is such that the entire 50-kilometre waterway dropped just 17 metres from source to city, a gradient of 0.034 percent maintained across a landscape that includes ravines, hills and this river crossing.

"Go early in the morning, before the coaches arrive. At seven in June, the light comes from the east across the Gardon and the stone is still cool."
La Vallette Boutique, Sagriès, 15 min away
When to go

Timing your visit

The Pont du Gard receives over a million visitors a year. The difference between a transcendent experience and a crowded one is almost entirely about timing.

Best: early morning

7h to 9h30

Arriving at opening means the monument before the coaches. Morning light hits the stone from the east. Leave La Vallette at 8h30, park at Rive Gauche, walk to the bridge. You will have it to yourself for the first thirty minutes.

Good: late afternoon

17h to close

Tour groups leave by mid-afternoon. From 17h onwards the crowds thin significantly. The light in summer is warm and low from the west. If you are staying nearby at La Vallette, this is the easiest time to visit spontaneously.

Avoid: midday in summer

11h to 15h

July and August from 11h to 15h: the car parks are full, paths are crowded and the heat is intense. If you arrive at this time, go directly to the river and swim first.

Practical information

Tickets, access and logistics

Entry and tickets
The standard ticket covers access to the monument, the museum and the riverside. In high season, booking online in advance is strongly recommended. Check pontdugard.fr for current prices.
Parking
Two car parks: Rive Gauche (left bank) and Rive Droite (right bank). Both are paid. Rive Gauche gives the most direct route to the bridge. From La Vallette, follow signs for Vers-Pont-du-Gard. The drive is 15 minutes.
The museum
The permanent exhibition on Roman hydraulic engineering is better than it sounds. Well-designed, multilingual and illuminating. Allow 45 minutes. The scale models of the complete 50-kilometre waterway are worth seeing.
Walking on top
Guided tours allow access to the top of the aqueduct, where you can walk along the channel through which the water ran. The view from 49 metres over the Gardon gorge is extraordinary. Book this when you buy your ticket.
Swimming

The Gardon river below the bridge

The river Gardon runs directly under the Pont du Gard and is one of the best swimming spots in the region. The water is clear, relatively shallow in summer and cold enough to matter on a 35-degree afternoon.

The left bank has a long beach area, good for families. The right bank has deeper pools and large flat rocks, better for those who want to swim seriously with the bridge immediately overhead. On a hot day in July, the river is as much the reason to visit as the monument itself.

The light show

Figuration Libre, summer evenings

From late May through 1 November, the Pont du Gard is illuminated each night after dark with a large-scale projection show. The images run across the full face of the bridge and change each year.

Park at Rive Gauche after 19h, walk to the riverbank and find a spot. The show begins at nightfall, around 21h30 in June. Bring something to sit on. From La Vallette, it is a fifteen-minute drive each way and costs nothing beyond the fuel.

How to make a full day

Combining the Pont du Gard with the region

A

Pont du Gard and Uzès

Morning at the bridge at opening. Swim until noon. Drive to Uzès for lunch on the Place aux Herbes. Afternoon in the old town. Fifteen minutes between the two.

B

Pont du Gard and the Gorges

Take the road north from the bridge into the Gorges du Gardon. The canyon landscape is extraordinary, mostly unvisited and accessible on foot or by kayak from Collias.

C

Pont du Gard and Nîmes

Thirty minutes separates the bridge from the Roman city of Nîmes: the best-preserved Roman arena in the world, the Maison Carrée temple, the Jardins de la Fontaine.

D

Evening light show and dinner

Drive to the bridge at 19h. Find a spot on the riverbank. Watch the show from 21h30. Return to La Vallette and eat on the terrace. A complete evening that costs almost nothing.
