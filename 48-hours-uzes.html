48 hours in Uzès

Uzès repays focus. This is the version we would plan for ourselves: specific times, specific places, and opinions about what matters and what can be skipped. Base: La Valette Boutique, 5 minutes south.

Jan-Willem & Fleur
Hosts at La Valette Boutique · Updated June 2026
Saturday · 8h30

The market on Place aux Herbes

Arrive before 9h30. The Saturday market on the Place aux Herbes has been running since 1241. It fills the main square and several of the streets behind it. The producers arrive early; the best things — the Cévennes cheese, the seasonal fruit, the Gard olive oil — go before 10h30. Buy first, then take a table under the arcades with a coffee and whatever you have just bought. This is the correct order of operations.

Saturday · 10h–13h

The old town on foot

Uzès is compact. The old town — built from pale golden limestone that looks best in morning light — takes three to four hours to walk properly. The ducal complex, the Tour Fenestrelle (the only cylindrical Romanesque tower in France), the medieval garden behind the cathedral, the Eure valley below the town where the Roman aqueduct source was. The tourist office on the square has a free walking map. Most of what matters is within 10 minutes on foot.

Saturday evening

Where to eat in Uzès

La Table d'Uzès, inside the hotel of the same name, is the most serious kitchen in the town. Book well in advance in summer. For something less formal: La Parenthèse on the Place aux Herbes serves good local cooking with a terrace that fills up on warm evenings. The bars around the square stay open late. Uzès is a town that eats well and without pretension.

Sunday · 8h

The Pont du Gard before the coaches

Leave the house at 8h. The Pont du Gard is 15 minutes away. The car park opens early. Before 9h30, the site is quiet — the coaches from Nîmes and Avignon do not arrive until mid-morning. The morning light on the limestone is different from the afternoon glare. Walk the lower tier, find the path that follows the Gardon upstream, look at the engineering from below. This is the version of the visit that people who have been twice prefer.

Sunday afternoon

Swimming in the Gardon or pottery in the village

Two options depending on the season and what you want. Option one: Collias, 10 minutes from La Valette, is the best swimming spot on the Gardon — clear river, rocky banks, fewer visitors than the Pont du Gard beach. Bring shoes for the rocks. Option two: Saint-Quentin-la-Poterie, 15 minutes north of Uzès, is a village whose ceramics tradition dates to the medieval period. The ateliers are still working — not souvenir shops but genuine workshops. Charlotte Casanas, whose tableware is in La Valette, works here.

Frequently asked

Questions about 48 hours in Uzès

What should I buy at the Uzès Saturday market?

The Cévennes cheese (from the smaller producers on the south side of the square), olive oil from the valley below Uzès, fresh pasta from the corner of the square. Buy early — the best things disappear before 10h30.

How long does it take to see Uzès?

A thorough walk of the old town takes three to four hours. A Saturday morning combining the market and the town is a full and satisfying day.

What is the best way to spend 48 hours near Uzès?

Saturday: arrive before the market closes at 13h, spend the afternoon in the old town, dine in Uzès. Sunday: drive to the Pont du Gard before 9h30, spend the morning at the aqueduct, swim in the Gardon at Collias in the afternoon. Stay at La Valette Boutique, 5 minutes from Uzès.

Do I need to book restaurants in Uzès in advance?

Yes, particularly in summer. La Table d'Uzès books up weeks in advance in July and August. Most mid-range restaurants accept bookings by phone on the day; walking in without a reservation in high season is possible but unreliable.
